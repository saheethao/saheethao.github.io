Time/Leadership

Roles/Conditions

This game is an adaption of "Two Rooms and a Boom."

Two Rooms and a Boom is a social deduction, hidden role game. There are two teams, the Red Team and the Blue Team. The Blue Team has a President. The Red Team has a Bomber. Players are equally distributed between two rooms (separate playing areas). The game consists of 5 timed rounds. At the end of each round, some players will be traded into opposing rooms. If the Red Team’s Bomber is in the same room as the Blue Team's President at the end of the game, then the Red Team wins. Otherwise the Blue Team wins.

In this adaptation, label each player as a random unique number from 1 to the amount of players. Insert all the values in to and your role should display on your device.

Randomly separate the players into two rooms. The “rooms” can be separated by a wall, door, or simply two open areas at a distance from each other. It is not important if players can see one another, but players in the separate rooms shouldn't be able to hear one another.

Start the timer at the specified time (see "Time/Leadership).

For more rules and specification, check out the rule book.

The first player that is nominated by another player to be the leader becomes leader. That player should take the leader card as a visual identifier for others players to see. It is important that players in a room can easily identify their room leader. If other players are not happy with this leader, they may attempt to change leaders.

Nominate a leader by pointing at them and if a majority agree then change leaders. A leader can willingly give up their leadership to anyone they so choose. A leader can change at anytime.

Leaders have the final say in who the hostages will be. Make sure you trust your leaders.

*Note: Leaders cannot be hostages themselves

For more rules and specification, check out the rule book.

Conditions

"Dead" - You are in the same room as the bomber as the end of the game.

"Coy" - You may ONLY color share even when a character's power might force a card share.

"Shy" - You may not reveal any aspect of your card.

"Leader" - You are the hostage leader.

Primary Roles

President - You are part of Blue Team. You win if you do not gain the condition "dead."

Bomber - You are part of Red Team. You win if the president gains the condition "dead."

Secondary Colored Roles

Doctor - You are part of Blue Team. When playing with the Doctor, the Blue Team has the following additional win condition: the President must card share with the Doctor before the end of the game or the Blue Team loses. At the end of the game, the President will be asked if he/she card shared with the Doctor. At that time both players will verify or deny having card shared.

Engineer - You are part of Red Team. When playing with the Engineer, the Red Team has the following additional win condition: the Bomber must card share with the Engineer before the end of the game or the Red Team loses. At the end of the game, the Bomber will be asked if he/she card shared with the Engineer. At that time both players will verify or deny having card shared.

*Recommended to play with the Doctor with the Engineer

Coy Boy ­ you begin with the "coy" condition. Players with the "coy" condition may ONLY color share even when a character's power might force a card share. A Coy Boy can’t card share, privately reveal, or publicly reveal their card or their color.

Shy Guy - you begin with the "shy" condition. Players with the "shy" condition may not reveal any part of their card to any player.

Spy/Spy ­ this is a special character card that is the color of the opposite team. This means, for example, that the red Spy has an allegiance to the Red Team, but their card is blue.

Secondary Neutral Roles

Gambler - Before the every reveal their roles, guess who will win (red, blue, or neither). If you guess correctly, you win.

*Played in odd numbered games

Butler - You win if you are with the Maid and the President at the end of the game.

Maid - You win if you are with the Butler and the President at the end of the game.

Romeo - You win if you are with Juliet and the Bomber at the end of the game.

Juliet - You win if you are with Romeo and the Bomber at the end of the game.

*The Butler, Maid, Romeo and Juliet work well in the same game.

Intern - You win if you are in the same room as the President at the end of the game.

Victim - You win if you are in the same room as the Bomber at the end of the game.

Rival - You win if you are NOT in the same room as the President at the end of the game

Survivor - You win if you are NOT in the same room as the Bomber at the end of the game

*The Intern, Victim, Rival, and Survivor work well in the same game.

Sniper - At the end of the last round, before all players reveal their character cards, you must publicly announce which player you are shooting. The selected player does not have to be in the same room as you. You win if the player you selected is the Target. If the player you choose is not the Target or the Decoy, then the selected player gains the "dead" condition. Note: a "dead" Bomber does not detonate.

Target - you win if the Sniper does not shoot you at the end of the last round.

Decoy - you win if the Sniper shoots you at the end of the last round.

*The Sniper, Target, and Decoy must be in the same game.

Hero - You win if you are in the same room as the President and Bomber at the end of the game. If you win, the both Red and Blue teams lose.

Villain - You win if you are in the same room as the President and the opposite room as the Bomber. If you win, the both Red and Blue teams lose

Robot - You win if the first player with whom you card share or color share fails to achieve all of their win objectives. If you fail to card share or color share with any players, then you lose.

Clone - You win if the first player with whom you card share or color share wins, succeeding at all of their win objectives.
